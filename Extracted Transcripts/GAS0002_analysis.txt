**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with severe sharp pain in the upper right area of the abdomen that started a few days ago, worsened three to four hours prior to the Emergency Department visit, and is rated as an 8 on a scale of 1 to 10. The pain is associated with nausea, decreased appetite, and fatigue. The patient reports no vomiting, changes in urinary habits, or bowel patterns. The pain started after a meal three days ago and has not been alleviated by over-the-counter pain medications.

**Objective:**
- Patient appears uncomfortable and in distress due to pain.
- No signs of jaundice or skin changes noted.
- No abdominal tenderness or guarding on physical examination.
- Vital signs stable.

**Assessment:**
1. Differential Diagnosis:
   - Cholecystitis
   - Gallstones
   - Peptic ulcer disease
   - Gastritis

2. Working Diagnosis:
   - Cholecystitis

**Plan:**
1. Order laboratory tests including complete blood count, liver function tests, and abdominal ultrasound to confirm the diagnosis.
2. Initiate pain management with appropriate analgesics.
3. Consider consultation with a gastroenterologist for further evaluation and management.
4. Advise the patient to follow a low-fat diet until further evaluation is completed.
5. Schedule a follow-up appointment to review test results and adjust treatment plan accordingly.
