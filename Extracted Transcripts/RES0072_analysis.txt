- Nausea
- Vomiting (yellowish-green vomit)
- Swollen neck
- Sore stomach

- Feeling hot and sweaty

- Strep throat

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a sore throat that started the previous day, making swallowing painful for both solids and liquids. They have experienced nausea and vomiting, with yellowish-green vomit. The patient also reports a swollen neck, sore stomach, headache, feeling hot and sweaty, and mild chest pain.

**Objective:**
- Swollen neck noted on examination
- No cough, sputum, or yellow material production
- No coughing up blood
- Mild chest pain reported
- No signs of respiratory distress

**Assessment:**
1. Possible strep throat based on symptoms of sore throat, painful swallowing, and swollen neck.
2. Gastroenteritis or gastritis suggested by symptoms of nausea, vomiting, and sore stomach.
3. Viral infection or acute pharyngitis could also be considered due to the sore throat and associated symptoms.

**Plan:**
1. Perform testing for strep throat.
2. Conduct a COVID-19 test due to the presence of multiple symptoms.
3. Take the patient's temperature.
4. Prescribe antibiotics if strep throat is confirmed.
5. Provide symptomatic relief for nausea, vomiting, and sore throat.
6. Advise the patient to rest and stay hydrated.
7. Follow up with the patient to monitor their symptoms and test results.
