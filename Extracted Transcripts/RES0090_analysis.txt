**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a chief complaint of coughing up blood that started a week and a half ago. The cough initially started as a normal cough and has not improved, accompanied by weight loss, chills, night sweats, and fatigue. The patient reports recent immigration from India, living in close quarters with a large family, and potential exposure to individuals with tuberculosis.

**Objective:**
- Patient weighs 110 pounds, indicating significant weight loss.
- Denies chest pain, racing heart, headaches, nausea, vomiting, changes in bowel movements, sleep or appetite issues, exposure to fumes, dust, animals, or pets.
- No significant medical history, allergies, loss of taste or smell, current medications, hospitalizations, surgeries, or family history of heart or lung conditions.
- Non-smoker, non-drinker, no recreational drug use.

**Assessment:**
Based on the patient's symptoms of coughing up blood, weight loss, chills, night sweats, and recent immigration from a region where tuberculosis is endemic, the differential diagnosis includes:
- Tuberculosis (TB)
- Lung infection
- Lung cancer

**Plan:**
1. Isolate the patient in a hospital room for further evaluation and testing.
2. Conduct sputum smears and cultures to test for TB.
3. Perform a chest X-ray to assess for active TB.
4. Initiate antibiotic therapy if TB is confirmed.
5. Contact public health authorities and notify contacts if TB diagnosis is positive.
6. Monitor the patient closely and provide supportive care.
7. Follow up with test results and treatment recommendations.
