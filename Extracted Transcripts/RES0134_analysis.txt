**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a two-day history of cough, shortness of breath, fever, night sweats, yellow phlegm, chest pain worsened by deep breathing, wheezing, fatigue, congestion, muscle aches, and low energy. Symptoms started around a day and a half ago and have been progressively worsening. The patient reports no improvement with over-the-counter medications.

**Objective:**
- Vital signs: Temperature not checked, but patient reports feeling feverish. Night sweats reported.
- Chest pain located centrally, sharp in nature, worsens with deep breath.
- Wheezing noted, new onset.
- Patient appears fatigued and reports low energy.
- No signs of swelling in extremities.
- No abnormalities noted on skin examination.

**Assessment:**
1. Differential Diagnosis:
   - Pneumonia: Considering cough, fever, yellow phlegm, chest pain, and shortness of breath.
   - COVID-19: Given the overlap of symptoms with the ongoing pandemic.
   - Acute bronchitis: Possible due to cough, wheezing, and congestion.
   - Asthma exacerbation: Considering new onset wheezing and shortness of breath.

**Plan:**
1. Conduct a thorough physical examination.
2. Order chest x-ray to evaluate for pneumonia or other lung pathologies.
3. Perform COVID-19 swab test.
4. Monitor oxygen saturation levels and consider oxygen therapy if necessary.
5. Prescribe antibiotics if pneumonia is confirmed.
6. Advise rest, hydration, and symptomatic relief measures.
7. Follow up in a few hours to reassess the patient's condition and consider hospital admission if symptoms worsen or if investigations indicate the need for further intervention.
