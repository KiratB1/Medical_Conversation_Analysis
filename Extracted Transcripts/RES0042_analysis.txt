**List of symptoms:**
- Dry cough for the past 3 days
- High temperature of 99.9°F
- Runny nose

**List of possible diseases or illnesses:**
- COVID-19
- Viral respiratory infection

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a dry cough for the past 3 days, accompanied by a high temperature of 99.9°F. The cough is described as annoying and persistent. The patient also reports a runny nose. The patient is on biologic medication for Crohn's disease and has a history of eczema.

**Objective:**
- Vital signs: Temperature of 99.9°F
- Dry cough noted during physical examination
- No wheezing, shortness of breath, chest pain, palpitations, or swelling reported
- No sore throat, changes in taste or smell, vision or hearing issues
- No weight loss, changes in hair, nails, or skin, difficulty swallowing, nausea, vomiting, changes in bowel habits
- No dizziness, fainting spells, confusion, or memory loss

**Assessment:**
1. Possible COVID-19 infection given the presence of dry cough, high temperature, and runny nose. Testing is recommended.
2. Differential diagnosis includes a viral respiratory infection due to overlapping symptoms.

**Plan:**
1. Conduct COVID-19 testing with a swab to confirm the diagnosis. Results expected in 24 to 48 hours.
2. Advise the patient to isolate for 14 days regardless of test results, including from their children.
3. Recommend staying hydrated, eating nutritious foods, and getting adequate rest.
4. Instruct the patient to monitor symptoms and seek emergency care if symptoms worsen significantly, such as high fever, difficulty breathing, or neurological symptoms.
5. Follow up with the patient after receiving test results for further management.
