**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a complaint of aching pain in the right side of the belly that started 8 hours ago and has intensified over the past 5-6 hours. The pain is described as constant and worsens after eating fatty foods. The patient also reports experiencing nausea, fatigue, and a decreased appetite. There is a history of similar intermittent pain after consuming fatty foods in the past.

**Objective:**
- Pain localized to the right side of the belly
- No fever, chills, sweats, changes in bowel movements, or urinary patterns reported
- No significant weight loss or recent infections noted
- Patient has a history of diabetes and takes metformin as medication

**Assessment:**
1. Suspected acute cholecystitis or gallstones based on the location of the pain, worsening after fatty meals, and associated symptoms.
2. Differential diagnosis includes peptic ulcer disease, pancreatitis, gastroenteritis, or gastrointestinal malignancy.

**Plan:**
1. Order abdominal ultrasound to evaluate for gallstones or signs of cholecystitis.
2. Consider blood work including liver function tests and pancreatic enzymes.
3. Prescribe anti-nausea medication to alleviate symptoms.
4. Advise dietary modifications to avoid fatty foods.
5. Follow up for further evaluation and management based on test results.
