**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with worsening shortness of breath, discomfort in the chest, productive cough with yellowish or greenish sputum, feeling feverish, and chest discomfort for the past four or five days. The symptoms have been progressively worsening over the past week.

**Objective:**
- Patient has a history of COPD, high blood pressure, and GERD.
- Patient is a smoker, consuming about a pack a day for 30-40 years.
- No significant findings on physical examination mentioned in the transcript.

**Assessment:**
Based on the patient's history of COPD, symptoms of respiratory distress, and productive cough with sputum, the likely diagnosis is an exacerbation of COPD exacerbated by a respiratory infection. Pneumonia and COVID-19 are also considered in the differential diagnosis due to the presence of fever and respiratory symptoms.

**Plan:**
1. Perform a full physical examination and vital signs assessment.
2. Order nebulized medications for home use to manage COPD symptoms.
3. Conduct blood work and chest X-ray to evaluate for pneumonia.
4. Perform a COVID-19 swab test to rule out infection.
5. Coordinate with the emergency department for further evaluation and management, including potential admission for treatment with antibiotics.
6. Educate the patient on smoking cessation resources and lifestyle modifications to improve respiratory health.
