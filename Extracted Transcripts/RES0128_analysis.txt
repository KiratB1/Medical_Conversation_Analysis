**List of symptoms:**
- Severe cough
- Wheezing
- Shortness of breath
- Dry cough
- Improvement in shortness of breath after stopping physical activity

**List of possible diseases or illnesses:**
- Asthma
- Allergic reaction
- Respiratory infection
- COVID-19 (ruled out with testing)

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
A 6-year-old male patient presented with a severe dry cough that developed during soccer practice, accompanied by wheezing and shortness of breath, which improved after physical activity cessation. The patient has a history of seasonal allergies and possible eczema. No fever, blood in cough, chest pain, or other concerning symptoms reported by the parent. The patient's sister has a history of asthma.

**Objective:**
- No fever noted
- Wheezing present on auscultation
- No signs of respiratory distress
- No abnormal findings on physical examination

**Assessment:**
1. Suspected asthma exacerbation based on symptoms and family history.
2. Rule out COVID-19 with testing due to overlapping symptoms.
3. Possible allergic reaction contributing to respiratory symptoms.

**Plan:**
1. Perform a pulmonary function test to assess lung function.
2. Measure oxygen saturation levels.
3. Conduct basic blood work.
4. Prescribe bronchodilators (short-acting beta agonist and inhaled corticosteroid) for asthma management.
5. COVID-19 testing with a swab sample.
6. Follow up in 24 to 48 hours for COVID-19 results and adjust treatment plan accordingly.
