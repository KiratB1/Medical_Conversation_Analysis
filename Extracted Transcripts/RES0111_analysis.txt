**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a sore throat for the past four days, accompanied by difficulty swallowing solid foods and developing a fever. The patient reports a hoarse voice and throat pain. No other significant symptoms reported.

**Objective:**
- Throat examination reveals redness and swelling.
- No cough, runny nose, chest pain, shortness of breath, congestion, changes in vision or hearing, palpitations, gastrointestinal symptoms, fatigue, weight loss, or other systemic symptoms reported.
- Patient denies recent travel or known exposure to sick individuals.

**Assessment:**
1. Acute pharyngitis with possible etiologies including streptococcal pharyngitis, mononucleosis, viral or bacterial throat infection, or COVID-19.

**Plan:**
1. Perform rapid strep test and COVID-19 test.
2. Recommend quarantine for 14 days or until test results are back and symptoms improve.
3. Educate the patient on the importance of vaccines, especially the HPV vaccine.
4. Follow up with the patient regarding test results and provide appropriate management based on findings.
