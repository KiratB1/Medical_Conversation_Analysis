**SOAP Note:**

**Objective:**
- No fever, chills, wheezing, chest pain, blood in sputum, wheezing, facial pain, sore throat, shortness of breath, eye redness, headaches, muscle aches, or urinary problems reported.
- No significant findings on physical examination.

**Assessment:**
Based on the patient's history and symptoms, the likely diagnosis is:
- Viral upper respiratory infection (common cold) or allergic rhinitis.

**Plan:**
1. Recommend COVID-19 testing due to the presence of cough and nonspecific symptoms.
2. Advise the patient to continue symptomatic management with over-the-counter medications like Tylenol cold.
