- Pharyngitis (sore throat)

- Laryngitis

- Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) or acid reflux (burning pain while swallowing)

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a sore throat, mainly at the back of the throat, describing it as a burning pain that worsened since last night. He also reports a hoarse voice, difficulty swallowing solids, chills, fatigue, loss of appetite, a gross taste in the mouth, and abdominal pain. The patient mentioned his girlfriend had a sore throat a couple of weeks ago, indicating possible exposure to infectious agents.

**Objective:**
- Fever noted during the visit
- Patient appears fatigued
- No skin rashes or changes observed
- No cough, difficulty breathing, or shortness of breath reported
- No recent weight changes noted
- No lumps palpated in the neck or under the chin
- Gross taste in the mouth reported by the patient

**Assessment:**
1. Pharyngitis or tonsillitis likely due to symptoms of sore throat, hoarseness, and difficulty swallowing solids.
2. Possible viral infection or streptococcal infection based on symptoms and exposure to girlfriend with similar complaints.
3. Mononucleosis considered due to fatigue and swollen throat.
4. Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) or acid reflux possible considering burning pain while swallowing and gross taste in the mouth.

**Plan:**
1. Recommend throat swab for culture to determine the cause of infection.
2. Prescribe symptomatic relief such as throat lozenges, pain relievers, and fluids to stay hydrated.
3. Advise rest, especially if diagnosed with a viral infection or mononucleosis.
4. Follow up in a few days for reassessment or if symptoms worsen.
5. Educate on proper hand hygiene and precautions to prevent spreading any potential infection.
