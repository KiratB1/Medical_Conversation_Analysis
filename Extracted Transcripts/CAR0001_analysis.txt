**List of symptoms:**
- Chest pain (left side)
- Sharp pain
- Lightheadedness
- Trouble breathing
- Racing heart
- Sweating (related to breathing issues)
- Pain worsens with laying down
- Pain worsens with deep breaths

**List of possible diseases or illnesses:**
- Angina
- Myocardial infarction (heart attack)
- Pulmonary embolism
- Pneumonia
- Pleurisy
- Pericarditis

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
A 39-year-old male patient presented with severe chest pain on the left side that started last night and has been constant for approximately 8 hours. The pain is described as sharp and worsens with laying down and deep breaths. The patient also reports feeling lightheaded, having trouble breathing, a racing heart, and sweating. The pain is rated as a seven or eight on a scale of 1 to 10. The patient denies any recent trauma, prior similar episodes, or family history of strokes or cancers.

**Objective:**
- No visible signs of distress
- No abnormal breath sounds
- No wheezing heard
- No neck pain or trauma
- Neck appears slightly swollen

**Assessment:**
1. Acute chest pain with associated symptoms suggestive of a cardiac or pulmonary etiology.
2. Differential diagnosis includes angina, myocardial infarction, pulmonary embolism, pneumonia, pleurisy, or pericarditis.

**Plan:**
1. Immediate referral to the emergency department for further evaluation and management.
2. Consideration for cardiac enzymes, electrocardiogram (ECG), chest X-ray, and possibly a CT pulmonary angiography.
3. Provide oxygen therapy if needed and monitor vital signs closely.
4. Advise the patient to avoid physical exertion and continue to avoid laying down until further evaluation.
5. Follow-up with the patient post-emergency department visit for further management and treatment plan.
