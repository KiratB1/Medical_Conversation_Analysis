**List of symptoms:**
- Chest pain while breathing
- Shortness of breath
- Cough (dry)

**List of possible diseases or illnesses:**
- Pneumothorax
- Pulmonary embolism
- Pleurisy

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with sudden onset chest pain while breathing, worsening over the past day, localized to the right side with a stabbing sensation. Shortness of breath has been constant since yesterday. The patient also reports a dry cough that started recently.

**Objective:**
- No reported fever, chills, night sweats, or other systemic symptoms.
- No history of heart palpitations, dizziness, wheezing, or blood in sputum.
- No urinary or bowel problems, abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, muscle aches, joint pains, or skin changes.
- No known medical conditions, medications, allergies, hospitalizations, or surgeries.
- Social history includes living in student housing, occasional marijuana and alcohol use, and safe sexual practices.
- Family history includes a sister with asthma, but no known lung or heart problems.

**Assessment:**
1. Differential diagnosis:
   - Pneumothorax
   - Pulmonary embolism
   - Pleurisy

**Plan:**
1. Perform a physical exam to assess lung sounds and vital signs.
2. Order a chest X-ray to evaluate for pneumothorax or other pulmonary abnormalities.
3. Conduct blood work to assess for any underlying infections or inflammatory markers.
4. Monitor the patient's symptoms and response to treatment.
5. Educate the patient on potential diagnoses and the need for follow-up care.
