**List of symptoms:**
- Chest pain on the left side
- Sharp chest discomfort
- Pain comes and goes in episodes lasting 20-30 minutes
- Pain rated at 4 out of 10
- Pain worsens at work or while driving

**List of possible diseases or illnesses:**
- Angina
- Costochondritis
- Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
- Panic attack
- Stress-related chest pain

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with chest pain on the left side that has been occurring in episodes over the last two weeks. The pain is described as sharp, rated at 4 out of 10, lasting 20-30 minutes, and worsens during work or driving. The patient has been under significant stress due to personal issues for the past couple of years.

**Objective:**
- No reported nausea, vomiting, difficulty breathing, or fevers during episodes of chest pain.
- No recent travel outside of Canada.
- Patient smokes half a pack to a pack of cigarettes per day and consumes 1-2 glasses of wine on weekends.
- No reported history of high blood pressure or high cholesterol.
- Regular exercise routine of running or cycling 3-4 days per week without chest pain.
- Family history of heart problems (father had a heart attack at 60).

**Assessment:**
1. The patient's chest pain is likely multifactorial, potentially due to stress-related factors, smoking habits, or underlying medical conditions.
2. Differential diagnosis includes angina, costochondritis, GERD, panic attack, and stress-related chest pain.
3. Family history of heart problems raises concern for cardiovascular conditions.

**Plan:**
1. Refer the patient to a cardiologist for further evaluation, including an ECG and stress test to rule out cardiac issues.
2. Consider imaging studies such as a chest X-ray to assess for musculoskeletal causes.
3. Advise the patient to quit smoking and reduce alcohol consumption to improve overall health.
4. Recommend lifestyle modifications to manage stress, such as relaxation techniques and counseling.
5. Follow up with the patient after specialist evaluation for further management and treatment.
