**List of possible diseases or illnesses:**

**SOAP Note:**

**Subjective:**
The patient presents with a cough for the past five days, accompanied by a sore throat, loss of taste, and fatigue. No history of similar symptoms before. Denies chest pain, wheezing, abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, urinary problems, and other systemic symptoms.

**Objective:**
- Patient appears alert and oriented.
- No signs of respiratory distress.
- No fever noted during the visit.
- No abnormal findings on physical examination.

**Assessment:**
1. Viral upper respiratory tract infection likely causing the patient's symptoms.
2. Rule out COVID-19 with a test due to overlapping symptoms.
3. Differential diagnosis includes common cold and other viral infections.

**Plan:**
1. Conduct a physical examination.
2. Perform a COVID-19 test.
3. Advise 14 days of self-isolation until test results are received.
4. Monitor symptoms and provide supportive care for the common cold.
5. Follow up with the patient to discuss test results and further management.
